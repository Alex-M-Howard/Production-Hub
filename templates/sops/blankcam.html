Blank CAM

Purpose

Purpose: Document the steps needed to program a part using Amada's Bend CAM software
Scope: Applies to new AND revised parts to be run on the Amada laser
Responsibility: Engineering: Notify programming/fabrictation of the new or revised part and include a PDF drawing and DXF file in the appropriate folders

Programmer: Revise/release the part for fabrictation to run on the Amada laser
Procedure:

Open Blank CAM:

1. Click the button in the top left of the screen, and click open


Open Previous Nest:

1. Verify you have the "Programs" directory selected in the left navigation pane



2. With the material your program needs in mind, look to the MaterialName column and find the material that matches your part
        NOTE: You can filter by gauge and material by hovering over the "MaterialName" column and clicking on the filter arrow

3. Click on any program you find that has the correct material and guage you need, and click  
Delete Parts on Previous Nest:

1. With your mouse out of bounds, click and drag across the screen, highlighting all of the parts
2. With the parts highlighted, right-click your mouse and select "Delete Part"
Delete Previous Scrap Cuts: 1. Click on the "Scrap Cuts" button 2. Click the "Delete All" button

3. Click the "Finish" button
Update Process Setup: 1. Click on the button on the left of the control pane


2. Change the sheet name to "N[PART #]". ex. N101507 3. In the comment input, enter the tab size and your initials 4. Change the quantity of sheets, if needed 5. Change the sheet size, if needed     To change sheet size, click the "..." button next to the size box
6. Click "OK"
Verify Margins and Spacing: 1. Click on the button on the control pane

2. Using best practices, change the spacing and margins (Left, right, top, bottom)

Import Part DXF: 1. Click the button in the top left of the screen, and click "Import DXF/DWG Part"

2. Navigate to directory with DXF 3. With DXF file selected, click "Open" 4. When the DXF/DWG Import Settings dialog box pops up, click "OK"

Drag part to nest: 1. When the part is imported it should show up in the part list on the right side of the screen.


If the part isn't showing up, click on the material and gauge label



2. Click and hold the part number, and drag it over to the nest

Process Part: 1. Double-click on part to enter the part editor


2. In the control pane, click in the control pane
3. Using best practices, verify that joints are in the correct locations and holes are correctly processed

4. When finished, click in the control pane
Populate nest: 1. Click, hold, and drag processed part into lower left corner 2. While holding the CTRL key, click and drag the part again to create a copy. You can hightlight multiple parts to make multiple copies

3. With the spacing and margins already set, you can drag copies to the edge of the sheet and against other parts to get as many as possible

Auto-space parts: 1. With parts nested against each other in the corner, click the button
If the button is greyed out, it is due to a part being selected. Click anywhere on the nest that isn't a part

2. If the auto spacing didn't evenly spread out all parts, you can manually drag parts around the nest to space them out
Add Scrap Cuts: 1. Click on the "Scrap Cut" button in the control pane

2. Click on the "Add" tab

3. WIth "Batch Instruction" clicked, the division number should be 3
If some of the scrap cuts are too directly on a part edge, change the "Batch Instruction" from "Division No." to "Pitch" and change the "X" value until the scrap cuts aren't directly on the edge of any parts

4. With the scrap cuts applied, click "Apply" and then "Finish"
Apply Nest Sequencing: 1. Click on the button in the control pane
2. Using the dropdown under "Edit Mode", use either the "Grid" or "Consecutive Instruction" options.


    Consecutive Instruction: click each part in the order you want
    Grid: Using either the X or Y axis, you can select how many rows/columns and the order will be automatically set
3. When the sequencing is done, click "Apply"
Create NC: 1. Click the button in the control pane
2. In the "NC Create" dialog box, verify that "Save" is checked and press "OK"


3. THere will be another dialog box with an alert of a sheet with a warning or error. This is due to a scrap cut interference on the bottom of the sheet that is unavoidable. Click "Yes"


4. When the "Save Schedule/Sheet" dialog box appears, click "Save"
Save Setup Sheet: 1. Click the "Print" button on the print preview that appears

2. Verify the printer name is set to "Microsoft Print to PDF" and click "OK"

3. When the save dialog box opens, save in the Amada Setups directory \\PRIZ-MERRYMECH\Amada\SETUPS and name your file the nest name and add the word "SETUP". ex. N101507 SETUP
4. Click "Save"
