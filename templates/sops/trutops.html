Tru-Tops

Purpose

Purpose: Document the steps needed to program a part using Trumpf's Tru-Tops software
Scope: Applies to new AND revised parts to be run on the Trumpf fiber laser
Responsibility: Engineering: Notify programming/fabrictation of the new or revised part and include a PDF drawing and DXF file in the appropriate folders

Programmer: Revise/release the part for fabrictation to run on the Trumpf fiber laser
Procedure:

Import Part:

1. In the Parts tab, Click the button to open the file explorer dialog box.
2. Navigate to folder with DXF file, and click open
Add Part Info:

1. In the part screen, enter  Name ,  Drawing Number ,  ID ,  External Name , and  Note 
Select Raw Material:

1. Click the button to the right of the Raw Material section
2. Find the correct material in the raw material window and press "OK"
Boost and Open Part:
1. Click the button, then the button

Check flattened DXF:
1. Inside of the tasks window at the bottom left, clearn any errors by hittin the "x" next to them, if available
2. Click the button in the top left of the screen, then exit this screen
Create Cutting Program:
1. On the right hand side of the "Cutting Programs" section, click and then underneath, the button

Select Technology Table:
1. When opening the cutting program, it opens a dialog box asking to choose the Tech Table. There should only be one table in the list, so double click it and press "OK"

Process all on single part:
1. With the part editor open, click the technology tab

2. Click the button
Add joints to single part:
1. Click the button
2. Click the button that pops up to the left of the part

3. In the microjoint box, select the "At contour end" option, verify the length of the joint, and click "OK"

4. Click on the outer contour of the part and any inner contours to create the first joint. Note: You can only do this on the most recent joint created
    4.1. If you need to move the joint that was created, click the button
    4.2. Click the approaching button that pops up to the left of the part

    4.3 Select the new location on the part to move the previously created microjoint
5. Now that the first joint is created, repeat steps 1, 2, and 3, but instead of "At contour end", choose the option "Random" and click "OK"

6. Click on the part where you want the reminaing joints

Create New Nest
1. Make sure the "NESTING" tab is selected and click button in the top left of the screen
2. Fill in nest ID. Nest ID will just be the part number with an N in front. ex. N101507 You can ignore the raw material

Fill in Nest Notes

1. Click the scrap cut button. Not the button that says "Scrap", but the one next to it on the top


2. Click on vertical sheet cut button



        If the vertical sheet button is greyed out:         Click out of nest, and then double click nest to go back in. Repeat steps 1 and 2

3. On the pop-up box, click "OK"


4. In the input box that pops up, type in the X-coordinates you want the sheet cut. For a sheet 120" in X, a sheet cut at 40 and 80 would be best, but might need adjusted slightly to make sure sheet cut isn't too close to vertical edges of the parts.

Nesting (Nesting Tab) - Set Auto Loader Suction:
1. Click



2. Select all of the circles that are half or more on the nest to ensure proper automation loading. The cirlces will turn from blue to pink when they are selected

3. Confirm your selection by pressing the checkmark button on the left

Nesting (Start Tab) - Verify NC Parameters:
1. Click on the dropdown arrow under "Create NC"



2. In the NC Parameters box, click on the Sequence tab and make sure that "Vaporize all" is selected for Stainless Steel, or "No vaporizing" for other material



3. In the NC Parameters box, click on the "Sheet Position" tab and make sure that "With height regulation" is selected. If running in automation, verify that "Pallet Change before MP" is selected, otherwise "Manual"

Nesting (Start Tab) - Create NC and Save:
1. Click on *Not the dropdown arrow*

2. After the NC is generated, click the button in the top left of the screen, then exit this screen by clicking the "x" in the top right of the window
